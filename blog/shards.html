Shards: Or, "Introduction"


So, here we are. The first article on “Shards”, and likely one that’s going to be pinned at the top of the homepage for a while, either to explain what this is about for future visitors or if I just get bored of writing and never write anything more. Therefore, let’s seize the opportunity and have a conversation about what this is, why it came to be, and what I hope it can become.

What is the blog for? As I say in the “about me” section, Shards is simply a way of putting thoughts I have into words and an excuse to dive a bit deeper into ideas I may have that I enjoy reflecting upon. Essentially, it’s a corner of the internet where all the ideas I’ve piled up and was not able to work into schoolwork will be realized at some point, from bits of writing to technical projects to photographs.

Why is it named “Shards”? It takes its name from what I would like it to be: not one coherent publication about a single topic, but fragments and pieces of ideas or thoughts I may have (shards, therefore, if you will) which I deem somehow interesting enough to write down as reflections or small research pieces. Don’t expect long, elaborate articles or long-form content in general: it isn’t what this is supposed to be about. I also particularly despise the linear format of essays (and I’m sure I’ll talk about this in its own article at some point!), so I see this as a way of breaking away from that, too.

Why is it hosted on my own website? This is an excellent question, and one I debated with myself about for a long time: this blog is hosted on a website of my own making on GitHub Pages. This is a bit strange for a website made by a complete nobody, as it essentially dooms the blog to complete obscurity from the get-go: without the ability to appear in someone’s “recommended” section on, say, Substack or WordPress, it will be tough to gain an audience and make it so that I’m not just screaming at a wall a thousand words at a time. However, gaining an audience and becoming “internet famous” really isn’t the driving force behind the blog, so I don’t mind the tradeoff between complete customizability and control over my own work and its resulting zero readers (in some way, this could be thought of as the 2021 version of the “open internet diaries” that used to be all the rage back in the mid-2000s). It’s also a statement to myself: being able to open a blog from scratch at no cost is a way of telling myself that I’m able to transfer the skills I’ve acquired to something entirely mine that I can enjoy making.

Why not just use the school newspaper? My high school offers a school newspaper to its students, and, initially, this was where I planned on publishing the ideas that eventually pushed me to form this website. I moved away from it (or, rather, never approached it) simply because I felt that it didn’t really give me the freedom to do whatever I wanted with content creation: having to adhere to a specific register and style of writing, and especially being somewhat limited by having to make sure the content was somehow school-relevant wouldn’t work properly with the ideas I have.

Who the hell do I think I am, and why are my opinions worth the computing power which could be going towards NASA’s calculations for the next lunar lander? As of now, no government agency has approached me about the power I’m wasting, so I think we’re in the clear. Should this change, I pinky-promise I’ll give the gigahertz back. On a more serious note, to be honest, as I’ve previously stated, this blog isn’t really meant to change anyone’s mind about anything or express some sort of secret truth to the universe. It’s simply meant as a creative outlet for yours truly, and if someone thinks my ideas are worth discussing, that’ll always be a plus (my email address is at the bottom of the page ;) ). Obviously I think what I put out is interesting -- I wouldn’t have taken the effort to write it out if it wasn’t -- but it’s also a fun side-project to take my focus off of school, so I would not look far past that.

So, what is Shards? It’s a place where I can put things out there for putting things out there’s sake, where I can write and post what I want exactly how I want it and get away with it. Hopefully you won’t hate it.
